## The Productivity Discontinuity

The enterprise landscape is experiencing a fundamental shift. AI capabilities are not following the gradual improvement curves of previous technology waves. Instead, we are seeing step-function improvements in enterprise productivity that are reshaping competitive dynamics across industries.

## Framework: Three Waves of AI Impact

### Wave 1: Efficiency Gains (2023-2025)
The first wave focuses on automating existing processes. Organizations that move early capture 15-30% efficiency improvements in knowledge work, customer service, and operational processes.

### Wave 2: Capability Expansion (2025-2027)
The second wave enables entirely new capabilities. AI-augmented strategy, real-time market intelligence, and predictive decision-making create new sources of competitive advantage.

### Wave 3: Business Model Transformation (2027-2030)
The third wave transforms business models. AI-native organizations emerge with fundamentally different cost structures, speed of execution, and customer value propositions.

## Enterprise Implications

Organizations must assess their position across three dimensions: AI readiness of existing operations, ability to leverage AI for new capabilities, and willingness to transform business models.

The window for establishing AI leadership is narrowing. Early movers in each industry are building compounding advantages that will be increasingly difficult to replicate.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Audit current AI initiatives** against the three-wave framework
2. **Identify wave-2 opportunities** that create defensible competitive advantage
3. **Build AI governance** that enables speed while managing risk
4. **Invest in talent and culture** for AI-augmented decision making
