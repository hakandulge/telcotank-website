## The New Compute Economy

AI semiconductor demand is growing at unprecedented rates. The competition between established players and new entrants is creating supply constraints, pricing dynamics, and strategic dependencies that every technology-intensive enterprise must understand.

## Framework: Supply Chain Strategic Analysis

### Design Leadership
The competition between NVIDIA, AMD, Intel, and emerging custom silicon players is creating a dynamic landscape with implications for enterprise AI infrastructure decisions.

### Manufacturing Concentration
The concentration of advanced semiconductor manufacturing creates supply chain risks that are being addressed through massive national investment programs.

### Export Controls and Geopolitics
Semiconductor export controls are reshaping access to advanced AI capabilities and creating tiered technology markets.

### Custom Silicon Trend
Major cloud providers and large enterprises are investing in custom AI silicon, creating new dynamics in the compute market.

## Enterprise Strategic Implications

Organizations building AI infrastructure must develop semiconductor strategy as a core competency. This includes understanding the supply landscape, managing vendor dependencies, and planning for evolving capability tiers.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Develop a semiconductor intelligence function** to monitor market dynamics
2. **Diversify compute suppliers** to reduce dependency risks
3. **Evaluate custom silicon** opportunities for specific workloads
4. **Plan for tiered capabilities** based on geographic access to advanced chips
